1 Estimates Are Not Final Prices

Every dollar figure shown anywhere on ElectriSolve — including AI-generated diagnoses, the cost estimator, and quotes sent by contractors before an in-person visit — is a preliminary estimate for planning purposes only. It is not a binding price, a guarantee, or an offer of a fixed cost.

The actual price is determined by the licensed electrician after an in-person assessment of the job. The final cost may be higher or lower than any estimate shown, and may change if the work turns out to involve more than was initially described — for example, hidden damage, outdated wiring discovered on site, additional code-compliance requirements, or access difficulties not visible in photos.

AI-generated estimates are based on the limited information you provide (a text description and optional photos) and on general market-rate data. They cannot account for the specific conditions of your home. You should never make a financial decision based solely on an estimate shown on this platform. Always confirm the final scope and price in writing with your chosen electrician before work begins.

2 On-Site Assessment Required

For most jobs — and always for installations, upgrades, and any work requiring a permit — a licensed electrician will conduct an in-person assessment of the job site before final pricing is set and before work begins. This protects both you and the contractor by ensuring the quote reflects the real conditions of the work.

ElectriSolve distinguishes between two types of requests:

  • Service calls / diagnostics — the electrician typically visits first to diagnose the issue, then provides a firm quote on site. Any estimate shown beforehand is a rough range only.
  • Installations & upgrades — scope is better defined up front, but the final price is still confirmed after an on-site review.
